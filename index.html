About Me

My story begins in January 2014.

I had recently left my job as a customer service representative with a local cable provider to go back to school. After a five year break from school, I still had no clue what I wanted to major in. I've always been good with Math and Computers, so I talked to an advisor at Tyler Junior College. She had stated that the Health Informatics Industry was starting to grow and that I should look into it. Turns out I have no interest in Health Informatics at all.

I still wanted to study some field related to computers, so I took an introductory to programming course during my first semester back. My logic: I thoroughly enjoyed learning Spanish as a second language in high school, so why not learn a computer (programming) language? I fell in love with programming.

After completing the introductory course in programming, I decided to major in computer science. I attained my Associate's Degree from TJC in May 2015 and transferred to TAMUC in August that same year to finish up my Bachelor's Degree. I'm currently a student with a wide collection of skills ranging from Web Development using HTML, CSS, and PHP, JavaScript, Spring, and MVC to Applicaiton Development using C++, VB.NET, and Java.
